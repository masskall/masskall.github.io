### Project Overview
Traditional cultural tourism often faces the challenge of monotonous visitor experiences and low retention rates among younger demographics. To solve this business pain point, our team designed a **Proof of Concept (PoC)** integrating Physical Tourism with an AI-powered Live Action Role-Playing (LARP) game in the ancient city of Chaozhou. 

*(This project won the 1st Prize in the Macau Regional Final of the National E-commerce "San Chuang" Challenge.)*

### Business Analysis & PRD Design
As the core Tech Business Analyst for the team, my primary focus was bridging the gap between cutting-edge Generative AI and viable commercial operations:
1. **Dynamic Narrative Architecture:** Designed user interaction decision trees where Large Language Models (LLMs) dynamically adjust the storyline and quests based on tourists' real-time choices at historical landmarks.
2. **Safety Frameworks & Logical Boundaries:** Drafted the Product Requirement Document (PRD) to define strict system prompts. This ensured that the AI-generated content remained historically accurate and culturally appropriate, preventing "AI hallucinations."
3. **User Journey Mapping:** Mapped the end-to-end tourist experience, from app onboarding to the final climax of the personalized story.

### Business Validation
We formulated a comprehensive business pitch, including target audience analysis, API token cost estimations, and projected ROI. The project successfully validated the hypothesis that gamified, AI-driven personalization can create a "Thousand Faces for a Thousand People" (千人千面) experience without requiring heavy physical infrastructure investments.

### Impact
This experience profoundly shaped my understanding of how technology serves business. It proved that a great Tech BA must not only understand AI algorithms but also possess the acumen to design products within real-world constraints (cost, compliance, and user experience).
